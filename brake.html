Optical-flow braking detection (no telemetry): plays the clip through once and measures the divergence of the image's motion field (forward speed), ignoring head movement and cornering. Braking = the field collapsing. Less exact than GPS — it can mistake corners/kerbs for braking — so prefer the GPS import above when you have a track. Auto-zones are editable.

Min duration 0.4s
Engine-pitch detection: brakes are quiet, but braking means lifting the throttle, so the engine note drops. The scan tracks the engine's harmonic signature (fundamental + overtones), which separates it from wind (broadband, no harmonics) and voices (wrong band, weak harmonics), and follows it continuously so a gust or shout can't yank it. The line is bright where the engine is confidently locked and grey where it isn't — drops are only counted in locked sections. Drop sensitivity sets how big a pitch fall counts. Brief dips that rebound are ignored.
